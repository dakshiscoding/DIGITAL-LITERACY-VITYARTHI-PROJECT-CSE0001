## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud has become one of the most widespread cybercrimes in India, largely due to the rapid adoption of digital payment systems. Unlike traditional hacking, UPI fraud mainly relies on social engineering techniques, where attackers manipulate users into willingly authorizing transactions.

A typical UPI fraud begins when the victim receives a phone call, SMS, email, or message on platforms like WhatsApp. The fraudster usually impersonates a bank official, customer care representative, delivery agent, or even a buyer or seller from an online marketplace. They first attempt to gain the victim’s trust by sounding professional and convincing. Often, they create a sense of urgency by claiming there is an issue with the victim’s bank account, a pending refund, or an attractive offer that requires immediate action.

Once the victim is engaged, the fraudster sends a “collect request” or a fake payment link. They then mislead the victim by stating that approving the request will result in receiving money, when in reality, it authorizes a payment from the victim’s account. In some cases, they also attempt to extract sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or debit/credit card details, which can be used to carry out unauthorized transactions.

The most common targets of such frauds include students, elderly individuals, and people who are new to digital payment platforms. These groups may lack sufficient awareness about how UPI systems function, making them more susceptible to scams.

The consequences of UPI fraud can be severe. Victims often suffer immediate financial losses, emotional stress, and frustration. Recovering lost money is difficult and, in many cases, not possible if the transaction has already been completed.

Therefore, it is crucial to stay informed and cautious. Users should always verify the authenticity of requests, avoid clicking on suspicious links, and never share confidential information such as OTPs or UPI PINs. Increasing awareness and practicing safe digital habits are essential steps in preventing such cybercrimes.
